Stephen Hawking

1942 - 2018

Introduction

Stephen Hawking was a renowned theoretical physicist, cosmologist, and author. He was born on January 8, 1942, in Oxford, England. Despite being diagnosed with a rare form of motor neuron disease (ALS) at the age of 21, Hawking made significant contributions to the fields of physics and cosmology.

Contributions

Hawking's work on black holes, the Big Bang theory, and quantum mechanics revolutionized our understanding of the universe. His most famous book, "A Brief History of Time," became an international bestseller and inspired millions of people to engage with the mysteries of the cosmos.

Awards and Honors

  • Albert Einstein Award (1978)
  • Copley Medal (2006)
  • Presidential Medal of Freedom (2009)
  • Special Fundamental Physics Prize (2012)

Quotes

"Look up at the stars and not down at your feet. Try to make sense of what you see, and wonder about what makes the universe exist. Be curious." - Stephen Hawking
